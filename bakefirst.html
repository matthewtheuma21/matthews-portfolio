At the core of this project is a manifesto that prioritises sensory experience and emotional engagement over literal visual representation. Rather than depicting recognisable objects, the work focuses on communicating internal qualities such as taste, texture, and atmosphere. Through minimal colour palettes, close-up compositions, and “straight-to-the-face” imagery, the book invites the viewer to feel rather than simply see. This approach challenges passive consumption, encouraging moments of pause, curiosity, and exploration.

These principles guided every stage of the design process. Appetising visuals were achieved through detailed, high-quality imagery that emphasises richness and texture, enhanced using tools such as Krea Enhancer and Photoshop. A structured grid system and consistent pacing were applied to maintain clarity and balance, ensuring the visual abstraction remained controlled and readable. The result is an immersive yet cohesive visual language that translates the manifesto into a physical reading experience.

The content of the book was curated with equal intention. Recipes were carefully selected to be simple, quick, and suitable for everyday breakfast preparation, focusing on accessibility without compromising appeal. By choosing dishes that are both visually engaging and easy to recreate, the book creates a practical and inviting experience for the reader, reinforcing its aim to make cooking approachable, enjoyable, and sensorially engaging.
