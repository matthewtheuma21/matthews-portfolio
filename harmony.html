This was another school assignment, and the given brief was to create visual posters for a music, culture and art festival that will be taking place in MFCC, Ta' Qali Park. For this project, I explored several design ideas using both vector and raster-based software, depending on what best suited the concept I wanted to develop. I created five different digital mock-ups, each reflecting a unique interpretation of the festival’s theme. The primary objective was to design a poster that would appeal specifically to families attending the event, ensuring the final outcome felt inclusive and representative of the festival’s atmosphere.

Some of my initial concepts focused on individual aspects of the festival—such as a music-centered poster featuring a hip-hop dancer, or a cultural approach with a speaker on stage. While each idea had merit, I found myself at a crossroads, unsure which direction would best represent the festival as a whole. I wanted something more all-encompassing that captured the full spirit of the event. Eventually, I arrived at a more general concept that involved masking a crowd photo within the word Harmony, stretched across the A4. Visually, it leaned toward a modern-grunge pop style. Although I was drawn to the black, white, and red palette (colours I often favour), the overall mood didn’t quite reflect the welcoming and family-oriented nature of the festival.

This led me to experiment with colour mapping. I shifted the colour scheme to blue tones—and that’s when everything clicked. The new palette brought a much friendlier, approachable feel to the design, making it feel far more appropriate for the event’s target audience. From there, I made additional refinements to the layout and typefaces, aiming to keep the poster clean, simple, and effective while still visually engaging.
