Zine Artwork (written by me) - A Snail Arrived By Mail

ZINE PREVIEW

for a school project, i had to create a zine taken from a poem or one written by myself, with the scope of showing a narrative visual. i chose to create a narrative myself, a poem about a snail that appeared to be doing its thing on a mail envelope, and gets killed by the postman who tries to remove its sticky mucus.

the message behind the story is very simple, i wanted to find a way of unexpectedly showing how killing snails just for their roles is an act of cruelty to the ecosystem. some people do unfortunatly, smash or throw snails at a large force just because they either do not want to touch the snail or because it’s stuck on something. so the way i structured the story of the poem, was to first explain a setting about a normal life of a snail, that is stuck on a letter sticking outside the mailbox, until it unexpectedly takes a dark turn.

A snail arrived by mail today,
Just how the snail got there,
I really can’t say.
But he’s a real smart one,
I hope he’ll stay.

I see the postman coming again,
As he grabs the envelope to re-check the stain.

Crickey! The snail is covering the address.
He waves the envelope up and down…
But the snail stays!
Sir Muller throws the envelope on the floor and lifts his knee up like a roar!

Should not have followed anymore…



this act of cruelty is not only unnecessary but also harmful. killing a snail disrupts the natural cycle it supports. moreover, it reflects a lack of respect for living creatures and the vital roles they play, no matter how small they may be.

instead of resorting to harm, we can choose kindness. the next time you see a snail stuck somewhere, take a moment to appreciate its place in the world. gently move it to a more suitable location where it can continue its important work. this small act of compassion can have a significant impact, both on the individual snail and on the broader environment. let us remember that every creature has a role in the intricate web of life. by choosing to protect and respect snails, we contribute to a healthier, more balanced ecosystem and set an example of humane behavior.
